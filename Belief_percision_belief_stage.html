Regressions - Percision in beliefs as a function of subjects’ average emotions

\(Y_{i,t} = C_{i} + \beta_1Neutrality_{i,t} + \beta_2Happiness_{i,t} + \beta_3Sadness_{i,t} + \beta_4Anger_{i,t} + \beta_5Surprise_{i,t} + \beta_6Fear_{i,t} + \beta_7Disgust_{i,t}\) (Fixed effects regression model)

\(Y_{i,t} = |\frac{Belief_{i,t,k=0}-Price_{t}}{Price_{t}}|\) (only 1 period percision).

Each emotion is averaged over 10 s’, during belief stage in period t, per each subject.

This calculation is considering the belief stage’s emotion, while the depending value is shown only in the record stage. Meaning: “How does the subject emotion during the belief stage contribute to the subject precision.”
